7 Side Hustles Remote Workers Can Start with $100 or Less

Explore 7 practical side hustles for remote workers that cost under $100 to start and offer quick returns within 3 months.

Side Hustles • Updated: 2026-03-18 03:10:25

TL;DR

Explore 7 practical side hustles requiring under $100 to start, with clear insights on potential earnings and common mistakes to avoid.

Who Should Consider These Affordable Side Hustles?

If you're a remote worker feeling the sting of rising living costs, you’re not alone. Many remote professionals seek extra income but worry about startup investments. With living expenses increasing, it’s essential to find effective ways to supplement your income.

Affordable side hustles can provide the financial breathing room you need without breaking the bank. These opportunities are especially relevant for those already working from home, such as freelancers, consultants, and remote employees, enabling flexibility while generating additional revenue.

Why Common Side Hustle Advice Overlooks Budget Constraints

Many pieces of side hustle advice highlight seemingly lucrative opportunities, but they often fail to consider the budget constraints faced by many remote workers. For instance, guides frequently suggest starting an e-commerce store or investing in stocks, each requiring initial capital often exceeding $500.

This type of guidance can mislead workers who can only allocate $100 or less. Moreover, the emotional impact of pursuing an expensive side hustle can be disheartening, especially if initial efforts yield little to no returns, leading to feelings of failure.

These scenarios can make potential side hustlers hesitant to try at all. It’s critical to challenge the assumption that successful side hustles necessitate sizable investments.

7 Budget-Friendly Side Hustles to Start Now

Here’s a list of seven actionable side hustles that remote workers can dive into with just $100 or less:

  1. Print on Demand: Start an online store through platforms like Redbubble or Teespring.

Initial investment is low—mainly marketing costs. Earnings can range from $5 to $50 per sale.

  1. Freelance Writing: For remote workers with strong writing skills, platforms like Fiverr and Upwork allow you to start offering services with no upfront costs.

Potential earnings can reach $30+ per hour.

  1. Online Tutoring: If you have expertise in a specific subject, online tutoring can be rewarding. Platforms like Chegg or Tutor.com require minimal setup costs.

Income can vary but typically ranges from $15 to $50 per hour.

  1. Affiliate Marketing: Join affiliate programs like Amazon Associates. A blog or social media presence can help drive traffic.

You can earn between 1-10% per sale through affiliate links.

  1. Social Media Management: Many small businesses look to enhance their online presence. With minimal costs for tools (like Canva for graphics), you could charge $300–$1,000 per month for management services.
  2. Dropshipping: Use Shopify to set up a dropshipping business without holding physical inventory. Its initial costs could be under $100. Your profit margins will depend on supplier pricing, typically between 10-30%.
  3. Virtual Assistance: Offer administrative assistance remotely through platforms like Belay or Time Etc. Initial costs are negligible, and hourly rates can go from $15 to over $35.

Real Success Story: From Remote Worker to Thriving Side Hustler

Meet Sarah, a remote project manager who embarked on her side hustle journey with just $75. Sarah knew she needed a creative outlet and a financial boost, especially with the holidays around the corner.

With her savings, she launched a print-on-demand store focused on quirky, customizable mugs and T-shirts. Initially, she relied solely on organic social media, sharing her designs in niche Facebook groups dedicated to gift ideas.

Within just two weeks of her launch, she made her first sale—an impressive milestone that fueled her enthusiasm.

As Sarah's designs began to gain traction on Instagram, her sales increased noticeably. By the end of the first month, she generated around $150 in revenue, and within three months, her income had soared to over $500 a month. One of her most successful designs featured a humorous quote that resonated with remote workers, leading to over 50 sales in just one week!

Recognizing the potential for further growth, Sarah decided to reinvest a portion of her earnings into targeted advertising on social media platforms, where she specified her audience demographics to ensure her ads reached the right people.

While it required time and careful monitoring, Sarah found that spending $100 on ads for one month resulted in an additional $300 in sales, showcasing a solid return on her investment.

However, she faced challenges like balancing her full-time job and her side hustle. Late nights and weekends turned into her prime working hours, leading to some fatigue. Nevertheless, she kept her focus on small, achievable goals. For example, she set a target of adding one new design each week, which helped her maintain momentum and keep her store fresh.

In one notable scenario, Sarah collaborated with a local artist to create a limited edition set of products, generating a buzz on social media that boosted her sales further.

Common Pitfalls of Budget Side Hustles: What to Avoid

While budget-friendly side hustles can indeed be lucrative, they come with common pitfalls. First off, many beginners underestimate the time commitment. You might think you can dedicate just an hour or two a week, but reality often requires a more significant investment, particularly in marketing and customer interaction.

Another mistake is failing to properly research the market. Without understanding demand, you may invest time in services or products that simply aren’t appealing. For instance, a failed dropshipping venture often leads to wasted funds on products that do not sell.

Additionally, financial miscalculations can cripple your potential gains. Beginners might overlook hidden costs—such as transaction fees—that slowly erode profitability.

Finally, avoid the trap of chasing trends rather than focusing on consistent income-generating actions. Often, the hot ‘next best thing’ leads you down a rabbit hole that distracts from establishing a steady revenue stream.

Your Action Checklist for Choosing the Right Side Hustle

Choosing a side hustle can feel daunting, particularly when balancing your primary job. Here’s a handy checklist to guide you:

  • Assess Your Skills: Write down your top skills, as this will help you identify which hustles suit you best.
  • Financial Capacity: Determine how much you can spend without jeopardizing your living expenses. Ensure your potential side hustle falls under the $100 threshold.
  • Time Commitment: Make a realistic assessment of how much time you can dedicate weekly.

Identify a hustle that fits within your available schedule.

  • Market Research: Spend some time examining your local market or online demand. Platforms like Google Trends can assist you in identifying what products or services have traction.
  • Set Clear Goals: Define milestones—what do you want to achieve in six weeks? Financial, skill-based, or creative expression? Having clarity will keep you motivated.
