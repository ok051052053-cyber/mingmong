Best Trading Apps for Beginners: Choose Wisely to Start Investing

Discover the best trading apps for beginners, compare features, costs, and find the perfect fit to kickstart your investing journey with confidence.

Investing • Updated: 2026-03-10 12:33:19

TL;DR

Best starter apps: eToro (EU/US), Fidelity (US), DEGIRO (EU) Fidelity is ideal for US beginners—zero commissions, robust support DEGIRO wins for EU investors with low fees, strong ETF access Robinhood is fine for micro-investors but limited for long-term goals Check fees, support, and research tools before depositing $100 or more

Quick Verdict: The Top Beginner Trading Apps Right Now

For new investors in the US and EU, the best starting point is usually eToro or Fidelity. eToro’s social trading platform makes learning less intimidating, while Fidelity combines low-cost ETFs with outstanding customer support. If you’re looking for $0 commission trades and simple mobile design, Robinhood is the US pick—but it lacks the investor education and safety net many Europeans find in platforms like DEGIRO.

Example: If you set aside $100 monthly, you’ll pay zero commission on many stocks with Fidelity or DEGIRO, while eToro also waives most deposit fees above $200. Timing matters: platforms with commission-free trades let you dollar-cost average without chewing up gains.

The biggest mistake? Rushing to the flashiest app, ignoring the painful tradeoff between ease-of-use versus long-term fee creep.

In summary: eToro and Fidelity are safe, simple starting points for US and EU beginners, with Robinhood as a gateway app for micro-investors content with limited research tools.

Who Each Option Is Actually For: Matching App to Goal and Experience Level

Not every beginner should end up with the same app. Here’s how the real split plays out after the first 30 days:

If you want social learning and follow-the-leader strategies, eToro makes sense. Its copy trading is relevant if you prefer to learn by watching others, and it’s available throughout Europe and the US (for crypto only stateside).

If your main goal is low-fee ETF investing, especially with plans to automate $100–$200 monthly contributions, Fidelity (US) or DEGIRO (EU) are the better fit. DEGIRO is best if you have a €2,000 or more starting portfolio and want wide market access without high recurring fees.

Example: A US student allocating 80% to a total market ETF and 20% to innovative stocks, in a Robinhood account with $500 up front, will see zero trade fees—great for nimble learning, but limited long-term account features. Meanwhile, new UK or German investors get DEGIRO’s lower commissions but face more setup paperwork.

Tradeoff: Apps like Robinhood are easy at first, but many users regret a lack of research tools after a month of real use. The right decision depends on your habit: monthly auto-deposits, social learning, or active short-term trades.

Where the Differences Start to Matter: Cost, Platform Support, and Learning Features

The friction between platforms often doesn’t become clear until you use them side by side. For example, Robinhood feels frictionless until you realize after 30 days there’s no tax documentation for EU residents and minimal education. DEGIRO shines with wide European asset coverage, but beware the €2.50 annual custody fee and per-exchange charges that eat away small portfolios.

In practice: eToro charges no real commissions on stocks but takes a 0.5% currency conversion fee and small withdrawal fees. These hidden costs matter when trading US stocks from the EU. On the flip side, Fidelity’s zero-commission ETFs come with a robust educational portal, which beats other apps for absolute beginners trying to avoid classic mistake—overtrading or frequent buying/selling.

A Real Setup Scenario: Starting With $100 or €100 as a Beginner

Let’s walk through the actual steps for a US-based beginner with $100, aiming to buy an S&P 500 ETF and a single favorite stock: 1. Download Fidelity’s app, verify identity (takes 1–2 days for approval). 2. Connect a bank account, transfer $100 (can be instant, sometimes up to 3 days). 3. Use the in-app search to buy $80 of an S&P 500 ETF (e.g., FNILX with 0% expense ratio), then $20 of Apple (AAPL), both with no trading commission. 4. Set up a recurring auto-deposit for the next month—Fidelity requires no minimum, Robinhood also allows $1 to start.

Scenario: A new EU user at DEGIRO starts with €100, registers with a passport photo, and waits 2–3 days for account approval. After depositing money, their €100 covers a commission-free ETF (say, iShares Core MSCI World), but per-exchange fees mean buying single stocks is less ideal at this size—fees may eat 1–3% per trade.

After 30 days, adding another €100 provides enough to diversify, though the interface remains less beginner friendly.

Decision: The right app setup isn’t just cheapest on day one. It’s the least likely to punish you with recurring costs or hidden withdrawal fees over a typical 90-day learning curve. Mistake: Many rush to deposit, skip small print, or try to trade actively before understanding day trading limitations.

Mistakes and Overkill Choices: Where Beginners Go Wrong and Why Simplicity Wins

A classic mistake is picking Interactive Brokers or similar advanced tools. These platforms look professional but overwhelm new users with tiered fees, unfamiliar order types, and margin prompts. Studies show beginners lose more than 5% of their capital in the first 30 days through accidental trades or chasing small swings, often after being lured by leveraged products.

Another misstep: Over-prioritizing zero commission while ignoring that apps like Robinhood monetize via payment for order flow (which can mean worse execution prices over time). The tradeoff is subtle: Is a $0.10 difference on a $100 stock material for you, or do you want the backup of comprehensive tax reporting (as with Fidelity or DEGIRO)?

In practice: Beginners often invest monthly, but get tripped up after the first withdrawal or when checking end-of-month statements. Hidden fees and slow support can sour an initial good impression. And for EU investors, some US-centric apps simply don’t work or carry major documentation headaches.

Decision: Stick to platforms you can genuinely navigate in under 15 minutes—and only upgrade to complex tools when you hit account limits or need international exposure. Simplicity trumps sophistication for your first 90 days.

Final Recommendation by User Type: Make the Right App Choice on Day One

Here’s the bottom line for real beginners:

If you’re US-based and want no minimums, quality ETF selection, and thorough support, Fidelity is the safest bet. For European readers, DEGIRO is the practical choice for low-fee, diversified ETF portfolios starting at €100–€200 (but expect simpler interfaces at first with eToro’s social features).

Choose eToro if you’re driven by learning from top investors, but don’t want to obsess over every fee—just be aware the 0.5% FX fee applies to cross-currency trades. Robinhood remains a starter option for $1–$100 micro-investors—especially if you want to experiment risk-free—but you’ll quickly hit feature limits if you get serious.

Example: An EU beginner who sets a starting allocation of 70% global index ETF and 30% blue-chip stock with DEGIRO, contributing €50–€100 monthly, will enjoy low maintenance fees and simple buy/sell paths. Overkill would be Interactive Brokers, unless you plan to trade options or need access to Asian or Latin American markets.

Remember: The right app is the one you’ll actually use weekly. Review your account after 30 days for hidden charges or missing tools, then level up. Avoid the mistake of waiting for the “perfect” platform—start, learn, and adjust as your investing confidence grows.

FAQ

What factors should I consider when choosing a trading app?
Prioritize regulatory safety, transparent fees, minimum deposit requirements, ease of use, and the quality of customer support. Also consider the available investment types—some apps focus on stocks and ETFs, others offer crypto or social trading features.

Are there trading apps with no fees for beginners?
Yes. Robinhood, Fidelity, and eToro (for stocks) offer commission-free trading, but always review the fine print—there may be FX fees, withdrawal charges, or premium feature upsells.

Is it safe to start with just $100 or €100?
Yes—most platforms let you start with small amounts. Look for zero or low minimum deposits and make sure recurring fees don’t eat up your returns. Apps like Fidelity and DEGIRO are friendly to smaller monthly contributions.

How fast can I start trading after signup?
Account verification can take 1–3 days depending on the platform and your country. US-based Robinhood and Fidelity accounts are faster, while EU options like DEGIRO may require additional documentation and manual approval.

What’s the classic beginner mistake with trading apps?
Jumping into overly complex platforms, ignoring the full fee list, or overtrading in the first month. Instead, start simple, automate deposits, and check all costs before trading frequently.
