Is FreshBooks or Wave the Best Invoicing Software for Freelancers?

Explore how to choose the best invoicing software for your freelance work, comparing FreshBooks and Wave to fit your needs.

Software Reviews • Updated: 2026-03-14 14:16:23

TL;DR

Choose Wave if you earn less than $30,000 as a freelancer and need basic features. Opt for FreshBooks if you're making over $50,000, requiring advanced invoicing tools and client management.

Quick Verdict

When it comes to invoicing software for freelancers, FreshBooks and Wave stand out. FreshBooks is often favored for its robust features and user-friendly interface, making it ideal for freelancers needing extensive automation.

Wave, on the other hand, is an excellent free alternative, perfect for those just starting out. If you expect to generate less than $30,000 annually, Wave may be your best bet.

Who Should Stay Free and Who Should Upgrade

### Who Should Stay Free and Who Should Upgrade

Freelancers operate across a wide spectrum, and the right invoicing software can vary greatly depending on the individual’s income level, workload, and client interaction.

For instance, if you're a hobbyist freelancer—perhaps a photographer or an occasional writer—making under $20,000 a year with just a handful of clients, Wave is an excellent free choice.

It allows you to create and send unlimited invoices, track expenses, and even scan receipts, making it perfect for managing small-scale projects without any financial overhead.

On the flip side, if you're a freelancer like a web developer or social media manager who’s scaling their business and earning upwards of $50,000 annually with a steady stream of clients, it might be time to consider an upgrade.

FreshBooks, for instance, offers features such as advanced time tracking, client management, and automated workflows that can save you countless hours each month.

Imagine spending just 15 minutes on invoicing instead of an hour, which can allow you to take on additional projects or enhance your service quality.

Take Jane, a freelance consultant who started with Wave, invoicing one client at a time and earned around $25,000 in her first year. As her client list grew to ten within two years and her income jumped to $70,000, she quickly realized that managing her increased workload became cumbersome.

Where Premium Starts Paying for Itself

### Where Premium Starts Paying for Itself

Investing in premium invoicing software can seem daunting, especially for freelancers just starting out. However, its true value often reveals itself during peak work seasons or when juggling multiple clients.

Take FreshBooks, for example: its feature for recurring invoices can save you up to 5 hours each month. If you’re consistently billing the same clients, that’s an hour saved each week—time better spent on delivering quality work or finding new clients.

Additionally, automation tools like payment reminders can drastically reduce the time spent on collections. If you send out 10 invoices each week and spend, say, 15 minutes chasing payments for each one, that adds up to 2.5 hours weekly.

With a typical labor rate of $15 per hour, that’s $180 spent monthly just on follow-ups. Incorporating FreshBooks could see you save around 10 hours monthly on administrative tasks, freeing up time that could translate into an additional $1,200 in earnings per year if you handle extra projects during that freed-up time.

A Real Setup or Switching Scenario

### A Real Setup or Switching Scenario

Imagine you're a freelance writer using Wave and generating around $25,000 a year. Your client base has grown from five steady gigs to ten, all eager for your services.

However, the increased demand means you're overwhelmed by the limitations of your current invoicing software. After researching alternatives, you decide to switch to FreshBooks to streamline your process.

The transition might take about a week, requiring a dedicated 3-5 hours for setup. You'll need to export your existing invoices from Wave, which typically can take about an hour, and then import them into FreshBooks—a process that usually takes another hour or two.

During this time, you'll also want to explore the new interface, which might require an additional hour of your focus to familiarize yourself with features like automated reminders and expense tracking.

To effectively manage this switch, a checklist can be invaluable. For example, ensure you've exported all invoices, checked for any pending payments from clients, and updated your payment details in FreshBooks.

If you're not careful, you risk double billing clients who are accustomed to Wave's invoices, which could lead to confusion and damaged relationships.

The time taken for client follow-up regarding billing discrepancies could cost you an additional few hours, so it’s crucial to communicate your transition to clients ahead of time.

Mistakes, Hidden Costs, and Overkill Choices

### Mistakes, Hidden Costs, and Overkill Choices

Freelancers often make the mistake of choosing invoicing software based primarily on cost, without a thorough assessment of their actual needs.

For instance, a graphic designer who opts for FreshBooks may only utilize a fraction of its features, which might be more than they actually require.

While FreshBooks offers robust capabilities and integrations, it comes with an annual price tag of around $180. Alternatively, software like Wave provides essential invoicing features for free — saving the designer a substantial amount of money without sacrificing functionality.

Another common pitfall involves the hidden costs of time and efficiency. For example, if a freelancer chooses an invoicing program that doesn't sync well with their accounting software, they may face invoice formatting issues and discrepancies.

This not only delays client payments by an average of two weeks due to back-and-forth corrections but can also lead to frustration that affects their productivity and, ultimately, their income.

If a freelancer averages $50 per hour and loses even five hours troubleshooting, that’s a $250 cost because of a poor software choice.

A scenario to illustrate these pitfalls: consider Sarah, a freelance web developer who invested in a subscription to a comprehensive software package laden with features she didn’t need, such as project management tools and advanced reporting—that she never used.

During her first year, Sarah spent approximately $600 on this software

Best Pick by User Type

Selecting the best invoicing software depends largely on your freelance discipline and how you manage clients. Designers often benefit from FreshBooks' robust tracking capabilities and project management tools, particularly if they invoice multiple clients at once.

Consultants, on the other hand, may find that Wave meets their needs by providing simple invoicing and reporting without hefty fees, but at the sacrifice of crucial tracking metrics.
